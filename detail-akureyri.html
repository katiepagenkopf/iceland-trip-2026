Akureyri

Capital of the North — cafés, botanic garden, and the best ice cream in Iceland

📍North Iceland, Eyjafjörður
Directions 🌿 Botanic Garden

What it is

Iceland's second city (population ~19,000), sitting at the head of Eyjafjörður — the longest fjord in Iceland. Akureyri has the feel of a real town, not a tourist stop: cafés, restaurants, a swimming pool, and one of the northernmost botanical gardens in the world. It's the natural base camp for exploring the north — Goðafoss, Mývatn, Húsavík, and Dettifoss are all within day-trip distance.

Good to know

You're stopping here to stock up on groceries (Bónus and Krónan are both here) and to explore the town. The Botanical Garden is free, compact, and beautiful in summer — Arctic and sub-Arctic plants arranged along paths overlooking the fjord. After walking the garden, grab ice cream at Brynja (locals will tell you it's the best in Iceland). Walk downhill past Akureyri Church for the best fjord views.

Icelandic Culture Note

The heart of the North
🇮🇸Where the Traffic Lights Love You
Akureyri's traffic lights are heart-shaped — a small detail that captures the town's character. It's the capital of North Iceland, the hub for everything above the highlands. Locals are proud of their independence from Reykjavík, their football team, and their swimming pool culture. The Botanical Garden, founded in 1912, is one of the northernmost in the world and showcases over 7,000 plant species.
