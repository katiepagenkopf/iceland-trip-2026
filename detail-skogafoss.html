Day 3

Skógafoss

A curtain of mist and rainbows

📍Skógar, South Coast
Directions Photos

What it is

One of Iceland's biggest and most powerful waterfalls — 200 feet tall, 82 feet wide, and almost always throwing a rainbow. A metal staircase of about 500 steps climbs the eastern cliff to a viewing platform on top, where the Skógá river rolls off the plateau's edge and the whole South Coast opens out below.

Good to know

The climb up the staircase is steep and often wind-whipped — take water. The viewing platform is the only way to see the falls from above. The lower path is wide, paved, and stroller-friendly.

Icelandic Culture Note

A chest of gold, lost in the spray
🇮🇸The Treasure Behind the Falls
Local legend says Þrasi Þórólfsson, one of the first Viking settlers of the area, buried a chest of gold behind Skógafoss. Centuries later, in the 1600s, a local boy is said to have found the chest and pulled a single gold ring from it before it sank back into the rock. That ring — if it's real — was eventually nailed to the door of Skógar church and is now in the Skógar museum.

The Story Behind It

On bright afternoons, the mist produces a single rainbow on most days and a double rainbow on the best ones — you can see the full arch from the lower path.
