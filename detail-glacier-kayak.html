Vatnajökull Glacier Kayak

Paddle between icebergs at the edge of Europe

📍Heinaberg, near Höfn
Directions Photos

What it is

A guided kayak tour in a glacial lagoon at the foot of Vatnajökull, run by Ice Guide. You paddle between blue-white icebergs that have calved off the glacier, in single or double sea kayaks. The full trip is about 3.5 hours, including a safety briefing and dry-suit fitting. All gear — kayak, dry suit, paddle, life vest — is provided.

Good to know

Wear long thermal underwear and wool socks under the dry suit — it's still cold even with the suit. Bring a waterproof phone case if you want photos. The lagoon water is 32 to 36°F year-round; the dry suit is non-negotiable.

Icelandic Culture Note

A glacier with volcanoes under it
🇮🇸Fire Under the Ice
Vatnajökull covers about 3,100 square miles — and several active volcanoes sit directly under it. In 1996, the Gjálp eruption melted through the ice and triggered a catastrophic flood (a jökulhlaup) that destroyed a bridge on Route 1 and washed away a chunk of the coastal road. Icelanders now monitor the glacier constantly for the next one.

The Story Behind It

From the kayak you can hear the ice. Small bergs pop and hiss as trapped air bubbles escape — a sound like a cold soda being opened underwater.
